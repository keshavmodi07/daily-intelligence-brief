# Builder Intelligence Brief

## Today's Alpha

- **OpenAI launches GPT-5.6 as preferred model in Microsoft 365 Copilot**  
  OpenAI announced GPT-5.6 as the new preferred AI model powering Microsoft 365 Copilot across Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Chat, and Cowork. GPT-5.6 offers stronger AI capabilities, improved cost-performance, and enhanced reasoning for complex tasks, enabling users to create higher-quality work products with less effort. This marks a significant step in AI-assisted productivity at scale (OpenAI News, [link](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-6-preferred-model-microsoft-365-copilot)).

- **Together AI closes $800M Series C to scale open-source AI infrastructure**  
  Together AI secured $800 million in Series C funding led by Aramco Ventures, NVIDIA, and Vista Equity. The capital will expand compute capacity and accelerate development of their AI platform focused on open-source AI, efficient inference, and AI agents. This underscores the growing importance of accessible, production-scale AI infrastructure (Together AI Blog, [link](https://www.together.ai/blog/announcing-our-series-c)).

- **SpaceXAI launches Grok 4.5, advanced AI model for coding and agentic tasks**  
  SpaceXAI unveiled Grok 4.5, its most advanced AI model trained on extensive datasets in coding, science, engineering, and math. Grok 4.5 excels at real engineering tasks and agentic workflows, trained on tens of thousands of NVIDIA GB300 GPUs. It promises to accelerate software development and AI integration in engineering (xAI News, [link](https://x.ai/news/grok-4-5)).

- **Tata Electronics and ASML advance Dholera 300mm semiconductor fab collaboration**  
  Tata Electronics and ASML signed an MoU to collaborate on establishing and ramping up a 300mm semiconductor fab in Dholera, Gujarat, India. ASML will supply advanced lithography tools and support talent development and supply chain initiatives. This is a major boost to India’s semiconductor manufacturing ecosystem and technology sovereignty (ASML Newsroom, [link](https://www.asml.com/en/news/press-releases/2026/tata-electronics-and-asml-announce-strategic-partnership)).

- **NASA astronaut Anil Menon to launch to ISS on July 14, 2026**  
  NASA announced that astronaut Anil Menon will launch aboard Soyuz MS-29 to the International Space Station on July 14, 2026, joining Expedition 74. Menon will conduct scientific research and technology demonstrations during an eight-month mission, enhancing India’s presence in human spaceflight (NASA, [link](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-sets-coverage-for-astronaut-anil-menon-launch-to-space-station/)).

- **Lovable in talks to raise $300M at $13.2B valuation**  
  Swedish startup Lovable, which enables software creation through natural language ("vibe coding"), is reportedly negotiating a $300 million funding round at a $13.2 billion valuation, doubling from $6.6 billion six months ago. The round is expected to be led by Menlo Ventures, signaling strong investor confidence in AI-driven no-code/low-code platforms (TechCrunch Startups, [link](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/08/lovable-reportedly-in-talks-to-double-its-valuation-to-13-2b/)).

- **UN High Commissioner calls for ceasefire in eastern DRC conflict**  
  Volker Türk urged the DRC armed forces and Rwanda-backed M23 group to stop hostilities amid ongoing civilian casualties despite a ceasefire agreement. The conflict has displaced over eight million people and involves heavy artillery and drone use, posing regional instability risks (UN News, [link](https://news.un.org/feed/view/en/story/2026/07/1167906)).

- **US Federal Reserve announces leadership of monetary policy task forces**  
  The Fed unveiled five task forces co-led by external experts to independently assess and recommend improvements in monetary policy tools and methods to better achieve price stability and employment goals. This reflects adaptation to evolving economic challenges (US Fed, [link](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260709a.htm)).
