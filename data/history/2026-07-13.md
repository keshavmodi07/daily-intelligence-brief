## Today's Alpha

- **Tata Electronics and ASML sign MoU to advance semiconductor manufacturing ecosystem in India**  
  Tata Electronics and ASML have formalized a strategic partnership to establish and ramp up a 300 mm semiconductor fab in Dholera, Gujarat. This collaboration includes deploying ASML’s advanced lithography tools and jointly developing domestic talent and supply chains. This marks a significant step toward India’s semiconductor self-reliance and supply chain resilience (ASML Newsroom, [link](https://www.asml.com/en/news/press-releases/2026/tata-electronics-and-asml-announce-strategic-partnership)).

- **SpaceXAI launches Grok 4.5, its smartest AI model for coding and agentic tasks**  
  SpaceXAI announced Grok 4.5, an advanced AI model trained on extensive coding, science, engineering, and math datasets using tens of thousands of NVIDIA GB300 GPUs. Grok 4.5 outperforms leading competitors on real engineering tasks, promising to accelerate software development and automation (xAI News, [link](https://x.ai/news/grok-4-5)).

- **Together AI announces $800 million Series C funding and secures 500 MW compute capacity**  
  Together AI raised $800 million from investors including Aramco Ventures, NVIDIA, and Vista Equity, alongside commitments for over 500 MW of compute capacity. The company focuses on open-source AI and efficient inference for production workloads, signaling strong growth in scalable AI infrastructure (Together AI Blog, [link](https://www.together.ai/blog/announcing-our-series-c)).

- **Samsung begins mass production of PM1763 SSD optimized for next-generation AI infrastructure**  
  Samsung Electronics started mass production of the PM1763 PCIe 6.0 enterprise SSD, optimized for AI and HPC servers. Featuring 9th-gen V-NAND, a 4nm controller, and liquid-cooling optimization, it offers high-speed data transfer and power efficiency in capacities up to 16TB (Samsung Semiconductor Newsroom, [link](https://news.samsungsemiconductor.com/global/samsung-begins-mass-production-of-pm1763-ssd-optimized-for-next-generation-ai-infrastructure)).

- **UN chief urges Iran and US to urgently resume negotiations amid escalating Gulf strikes**  
  Renewed military strikes between Iran and the US in the Gulf have escalated tensions, with attacks targeting strategic locations including the Strait of Hormuz and US bases in Jordan. The UN Secretary-General called for restraint to avoid wider conflict and disruption of global energy markets (UN News, [link](https://news.un.org/feed/view/en/story/2026/07/1167923)).
