## Today's Alpha

- **OpenAI and Broadcom Unveil Jalapeño, a Custom AI Chip Optimized for LLM Inference**  
  OpenAI and Broadcom jointly announced Jalapeño, OpenAI's first Intelligence Processor designed specifically for large language model (LLM) inference. Early engineering samples running GPT-5.3-Codex-Spark workloads demonstrate promising performance per watt improvements over current state-of-the-art solutions. This marks a significant step in AI hardware innovation, enabling faster, more efficient LLM inference and reducing reliance on third-party hardware (OpenAI News, https://openai.com/index/openai-broadcom-jalapeno-inference-chip).

- **NASA Artemis II Mission Success Shapes Moon to Mars Exploration Path**  
  Following the successful return of Artemis II's Orion spacecraft, NASA officials highlighted the mission's role in advancing lunar surface missions and crewed Mars exploration. The 10-day crewed lunar orbit demonstrated critical deep space capabilities, reinforcing NASA's long-term human spaceflight strategy (NASA, https://www.nasa.gov/centers-and-facilities/johnson/nasa-at-the-ion-orion-lessons-from-artemis-ii-shape-nasas-moon-to-mars-path/).

- **NVIDIA Advances Specialized AI Agents and Autonomous Telecom Network Operations**  
  NVIDIA launched the NVIDIA Agent Toolkit, an open modular foundation for building secure, specialized AI agents for complex enterprise workflows. Demonstrations included AI-driven autonomous telecom network operations using synthetic data and domain-specific models. NVIDIA also powers over 400 of the world's 500 fastest supercomputers, underscoring its AI infrastructure dominance (Nvidia Blog, https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/nvidia-agent-toolkit-open-models-tools-skills-secure-runtime-ai-agents/).
