## Today's Alpha

**UN Launches First Global Independent Scientific Assessment on AI Opportunities and Risks**  
On July 1, 2026, the United Nations unveiled the first-ever global, independent scientific assessment on artificial intelligence, providing an evidence-based framework covering AI’s opportunities, risks, and impacts across seven key domains. The report urges urgent global governance and shared regulatory rules to harness AI benefits while mitigating harms, emphasizing that the window for effective regulation is open but limited (UN News, [link1](https://news.un.org/feed/view/en/story/2026/07/1167853), [link2](https://news.un.org/feed/view/en/story/2026/07/1167848)).

**NASA Launches LINK Robotic Servicing Spacecraft to Extend Swift Observatory Life**  
On July 2, 2026, NASA launched the LINK spacecraft, built by Katalyst Space, to rendezvous with and boost the orbit of the Neil Gehrels Swift Observatory, preventing its atmospheric re-entry later this year. This marks the first commercial robotic servicing mission for an uncrewed NASA spacecraft not originally designed for servicing, demonstrating a new model for extending space asset lifespans (NASA, [link](https://www.nasa.gov/image-article/link-spacecraft-set-for-mission-to-boost-nasas-swift-observatory/)).
