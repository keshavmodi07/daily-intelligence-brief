## Today's Alpha

- **OpenAI launches Daybreak tools including Codex Security and GPT-5.5-Cyber to automate vulnerability patching**  
  OpenAI introduced Daybreak, a suite of AI-powered tools designed to automate the discovery, validation, and patching of software vulnerabilities at scale. Key components include Codex Security and GPT-5.5-Cyber. Partnerships with Trail of Bits, HackerOne, and Calif aim to support open-source maintainers in vulnerability management. This initiative shifts the cybersecurity bottleneck from discovery to rapid patching, enhancing global defenses for critical infrastructure and open-source software ecosystems ([OpenAI News](https://openai.com/index/daybreak-securing-the-world), [OpenAI News](https://openai.com/index/patch-the-planet)).

- **xAI launches Grok Build CLI and new autonomous coding features**  
  xAI released Grok Build, a terminal-based coding agent now in early beta for SuperGrok and X Premium Plus subscribers. Features include the /goal command for autonomous long-running tasks, an Agent Dashboard for managing multiple coding sessions, and integrations with open-source platforms like Kilo Code and Warp. A built-in plugin marketplace extends Grok Build’s functionality, enhancing AI-assisted software development workflows ([xAI News](https://x.ai/news/grok-build-cli)).

- **NVIDIA supports US National AI Research Resource (NAIRR) pilot with AI infrastructure**  
  NVIDIA contributed DGX nodes and Vera CPUs to the US NSF’s NAIRR pilot program, supporting 700+ scientific projects. This includes enabling agentic AI research at Los Alamos National Laboratory and powering Europe’s first exascale supercomputer JUPITER. NVIDIA also launched AI software tools accelerating research in materials simulation, astronomy, and more, accelerating scientific discovery across domains ([Nvidia Blog](https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/nairr-scientific-research-ai-infrastructure/)).

- **Rocket Lab sets record with 16-hour 42-minute launch turnaround for U.S. Space Force mission**  
  Rocket Lab launched the VICTUS HAZE mission just 16 hours and 42 minutes after launch notice, beating the previous record by over 10 hours. The mission deployed the Pioneer spacecraft, which is performing complex orbital maneuvers for Rendezvous and Proximity Operations (RPO) in low Earth orbit, enhancing space domain awareness and rapid threat response capabilities ([Rocket Lab](https://rocketlabcorp.com/updates/victus-haze/)).

- **Botswana to sign Artemis Accords at NASA Headquarters on June 25**  
  Botswana will become the 68th country to join the Artemis Accords, which establish principles for safe, transparent, and coordinated civil space exploration on the Moon, Mars, and beyond. This expands international collaboration in space commercialization and exploration ([NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-invites-media-to-botswana-artemis-accords-signing-ceremony/)).
